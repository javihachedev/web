In this project, I served as **Project Manager and Team Leader** for the software that supports user services for the Spanish Earth observation satellite, **PAZ**. My primary responsibility was to lead the operational maintenance phase, ensuring the continuity of a critical service for its institutional and commercial users.

## Critical Systems Maintenance and SLAs

I led the maintenance strategy with a zero-downtime approach, managing an environment where stability and traceability are imperative.

- **Lifecycle Management:** I coordinated corrective, adaptive, and preventive activities, prioritizing the resolution of complex incidents under strict configuration management standards.

- **Service Assurance:** Ensured compliance with SLAs (Service Level Agreements) and contractual commitments, maintaining optimal system availability through precise coordination between development and operations teams.

## Capacity Expansion and Contract Management

Beyond maintenance, I managed **expansion contracts** aimed at equipping the system with new functionalities.

> **Key Milestone:** I oversaw the entire cycle of new developments—from requirements analysis and effort estimation to execution—successfully integrating new features into the production environment without compromising the stability of the operating system.

## Team Leadership and Institutional Relations

As a bridge between engineering and management, my role required a balance between technical oversight and strategic vision:

- **Team Management:** I coordinated workloads and guided the team in making critical technical decisions, fostering a high-caliber engineering environment.

- **Stakeholder Communication:** I served as the point of contact for institutional representatives and stakeholders, providing visibility into risks, progress, and the mission’s strategic direction.

## Aerospace Impact and Vision

This experience solidified my ability to lead projects in contexts of **high operational criticality**, where contractual rigor and reliability are vital. My approach ensured that the system not only remained operational but also evolved in a sustainable manner.
